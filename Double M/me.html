About Me

I am Masaki Magack, a proud Pan-Africanist from Riakworo-Gesima, Nyamira County, Kenya. My academic journey has shaped my passion for communication, with a PhD in Media Studies (in progress) at Daystar University, building on my master’s in Digital Communication from USIU-Africa and a bachelor’s in Mass Communication from St. Paul’s University. I specialize in understanding how media, culture, and digital trends influence society, grounded in critical theories and Afrocentric perspectives.

Professional Experience

My career spans journalism, radio, and digital marketing, where I’ve served as Deputy Head of Radio and Head of Productions at USIU Radio 99.9FM, Marketing Communications Manager for several organizations, and a social media strategist. I’ve also hosted engaging programs like Morning Madness and Sports Zone, contributed as a co-host on Mambo Mseto, and appeared on Citizen TV, Nation TV, and KTN as a panelist discussing digital communication, youth mentorship, and Afrocentric issues.

My Work and Passion

As a researcher and digital enthusiast, I’ve contributed to global academic conversations, including a chapter in Black Communication in the Age of Disinformation. I am the founder of digitallke.net and co-founder of interflow, both platforms committed to telling Africa’s story. Through my work, I aim to blend media, academia, and business strategies to empower communities and highlight Africa’s rich cultural narrative.

When it comes to mentorship, Africa is lagging behind. I have become a passionate mentor for young African scholars and entrepreneurs, dedicated to fostering education, innovation, and personal development in Kenya. I believe that education and entrepreneurship are powerful tools for transforming lives, and I actively engage in initiatives that support and guide young Africans towards success.

What I Do:


  • YouTube Channel - Unlock your potential
    A show that speaks matters Afrocentricity On my YouTube channel, I explore topics centered on Afrocentricity, encouraging the youth to embrace education as a vital tool for improving their lives. The show features conversations with renowned African scholars and successful professionals who share their journeys, achievements, and the lessons they’ve learned along the way. These stories inspire young Africans to realize the transformative power of education and its role in shaping a better future.

  • Educational Mentorship for Young Scholars
    I provide mentorship to young African scholars through various platforms, including WhatsApp groups and weekly webinars. In these sessions, I offer guidance on academic research, helping students develop critical skills for success. I also conduct mock thesis defenses to prepare students for their final presentations, ensuring they are confident and ready to excel in their academic pursuits.

  • Entrepreneurial Webinars for Young Business Starters
    I offer free webinars tailored to young entrepreneurs looking to establish and grow their brands. In these sessions, I teach essential skills such as brand creation and digital marketing strategies. My goal is to equip young business starters with the knowledge and tools they need to thrive in today’s competitive market, using digital platforms to maximize their reach and impact.
